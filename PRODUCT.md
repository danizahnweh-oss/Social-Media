# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Students in grade 10, around age 16, playing individually on laptops or tablets during English lessons. They arrive curious, distracted, and used to polished online games, so the interface has to feel immediate and credible while staying readable at B2 level. Teachers use it as a flexible classroom activity where progress can pause and resume.

## Product Purpose

The product is a browser-based learning escape game about social media manipulation. It turns media literacy and English practice into a playable thriller: create an agent, enter the mission hub, solve ten missions, earn points and achievements, and reach one of several endings.

## Brand Personality

Tense, sharp, and encouraging. The world feels like a Resistance backchannel inside a dark social platform, with ALGO as a calm manipulative opponent. The tone should be modern and game-like, never childish, punitive, or lecture-like.

## Anti-references

Avoid worksheet-style e-learning, generic neon dashboards, plain quiz pages, fake app screenshots, decorative cyberpunk clutter, and marketing-page card grids. The game should not look like a school portal with a dark theme or a template landing page with quiz buttons.

## Design Principles

Make the player feel recruited within the first few seconds.

Every screen should answer the player's next action without visible instructions.

Use game feedback for learning: points, hints, ranks, achievements, and story beats should make progress legible.

Keep the engine modular: mission content comes from JSON, while UI behaviour stays reusable.

Respect classroom reality: sound can be muted, progress persists, text is readable, and every interaction works with touch and keyboard.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. The game must provide visible focus states, semantic controls, text alternatives for visual puzzles, reduced-motion handling, captions or transcripts for audio puzzles, colour-independent state labels, touch targets of at least 44 px, and no requirement to use a real name.
